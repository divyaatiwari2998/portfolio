Framework

What is RFM Analysis?

RFM is a proven customer analytics framework used in retail, e-commerce, and CRM strategy. It scores each customer on three behavioural dimensions, enabling data-driven segmentation without the need for complex modelling.

R
Recency
How recently did the customer make a purchase? Recent buyers are more likely to respond to campaigns.
F
Frequency
How often do they purchase? High-frequency customers show strong brand loyalty and habitual buying behaviour.
M
Monetary Value
How much do they spend in total? High-value customers contribute disproportionately to revenue.

Methodology

Step-by-Step Approach

01
Data Preparation
Loaded transaction data into Python using Pandas. Cleaned nulls, standardised date columns, and computed per-customer aggregates.
02
RFM Scoring
Calculated Recency (days since last purchase), Frequency (order count), and Monetary Value (total spend) for each customer using quantile-based scoring (1–5).
03
Segmentation
Combined RFM scores to classify customers into segments: Champions, Loyal, At-Risk, Lost, Potential Loyalists, and New Customers.
04
Strategy Design
For each segment, designed targeted engagement strategies — retention campaigns for at-risk customers, loyalty rewards for champions, re-activation for lost ones.

Segments Identified

Customer Segments & Strategies

Segment Profile Recommended Strategy
🏆 Champions Bought recently, buy often, spend the most Reward with exclusive offers, ask for reviews, make them brand advocates
💙 Loyal Customers Frequent buyers with consistent spend Upsell higher-value products, enrol in loyalty programmes
🌱 Potential Loyalists Recent customers with moderate frequency Offer membership or loyalty cards, provide personalised follow-ups
⚠️ At-Risk Previously good customers now inactive Send win-back campaigns, offer limited-time discounts, survey on experience
😴 Hibernating Low scores across all three dimensions Share valuable content, offer product refreshes, reactivation email flow
🆕 New Customers Bought recently but only once Provide onboarding experience, start building the relationship early
